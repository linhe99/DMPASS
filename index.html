Before the test

When should I get tested?

If you have cold or flu like symptoms, such as a cough, fever, sore throat, shortness of breath or runny nose, even if these are mild, you should get tested for COVID-19 as soon as possible. People with mild symptoms can still spread the virus. To help stop the spread of COVID-19, anyone with cold or flu-like symptoms should get tested.

Source:
https://www.webmd.com/lung/covid-19-symptoms?fbclid=IwAR2rqF_m579_y_fbLa-TgjBMiin_pNZ8lynBdubtP0p0gAl8tybbBfJjkvk#1-2

Where to get tested?

You can get tested in different places,
such as:
‣ At a hospital
‣ In your car at a drive through clinic
‣ At your local doctor’s office
‣ At a COVID-19 clinic
‣ Find the clinic near you from the NSW health website:
https://www.health.nsw.gov.au/Infectious/covid-19/Pages/clinics.aspx

Source:
https://www.health.nsw.gov.au/disability/covid-19/Documents/getting-tested-for-covid-19.PDF

What items should be brought when getting tested?

You need to bring your Medicare card and the details of your usual General Practitioner (GP) and/or GP practice, if you have one.

If you don’t have a Medicare card then bring one form of identification, such as your driver’s licence or student ID.

Source:
https://www.dhhs.vic.gov.au/taking-test-covid-19#is-the-test-free

Is the test free?

The coronavirus (COVID-19) test is free when you get tested at:
‣ NSW Health public hospital emergency department
‣ NSW Health public COVID-19 clinic
‣ Australian Government GP respiratory clinic
‣ Private testing collection service

However, the GP charges may apply if you visit a GP for a referral

Source:
https://www.health.nsw.gov.au/Infectious/covid-19/Pages/attending-a-clinic.aspx

During the test

What does testing involve?

Testing for COVID-19 involves inserting a 6-inch long swab (like a long Q-tip) into the cavity between the nose and mouth (nasopharyngeal swab) for 15 seconds and rotating it several times. The swabbing is then repeated on the other side of the nose to make sure enough material is collected. The swab is then inserted into a container and sent to a lab for testing.

Source:
https://health.ucdavis.edu/coronavirus/coronavirus-testing.html

Who can perform tests?

At testing sites, test samples will be taken by a trained healthcare worker, usually a doctor or nurse.

Source:
https://health.ucdavis.edu/coronavirus/coronavirus-testing.html

After the test

Quarantine and isolation

After your test, you must return home immediately and stay at home until you receive your test result. You should not see anyone that doesn’t live with you while you are waiting for your test result (self-isolating). This is referred to as ‘self-isolating’. You must do this because there is a risk that you could have coronavirus (COVID-19) and infect other people.

Source:
https://www.dhhs.vic.gov.au/taking-test-covid-19#what-does-testing-involve

Test result

Results typically take 24 to 72 hours. If you’ve been tested at a public hospital clinic or emergency department, you can also receive your result securely via:
‣ Your MyServiceNSW account
‣ A push notification, if you also have the Service NSW mobile app.

Source:
https://www.nsw.gov.au/covid-19/how-to-protect-yourself-and-others/clinics#find-a-covid-19-testing-centre-in-nsw
